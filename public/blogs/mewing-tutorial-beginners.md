# Mewing Tutorial for Beginners: Complete 2026 Guide

Mewing is the practice of proper tongue posture that may improve facial structure over time. This complete guide covers everything you need to know to start mewing correctly today.

## What is Mewing?

Mewing (named after Dr. Mike Mew) is maintaining proper tongue posture by keeping your entire tongue pressed against the roof of your mouth. Proponents claim it can:

- Improve jawline definition
- Enhance cheekbone prominence
- Correct forward head posture
- Reduce double chin appearance
- Promote forward facial growth

## The Science Behind Mewing

**The theory:** Constant upward pressure from your tongue can influence bone remodeling through Wolff's Law (bones adapt to forces placed on them).

**Evidence level:** Mostly anecdotal. Limited scientific studies, but thousands of before/after reports online show promising results, especially for younger users (under 25).

## How to Mew: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Find the Correct Tongue Position

1. **Say "sing"** - notice where your tongue goes? That's the starting point.
2. **Swallow** - your tongue naturally presses up. Try to hold that position.
3. **The entire tongue should be up** - not just the tip. This is critical!

### Step 2: The "Mewing" Position

**Correct posture:**

- Entire tongue flat against palate (roof of mouth)
- INCLUDING the back third of tongue (hardest part)
- Teeth lightly touching or slightly apart (not clenched)
- Lips closed but relaxed
- Nose breathing only

**Common mistake:** Only pressing tongue tip up while back of tongue stays down. This doesn't work!

### Step 3: Find Your Back Third

The back third is key but tricky to engage.

**Technique:**

1. Make a "K" sound - feel back of tongue rise?
2. Make a "NG" sound (like "si**ng**") - back tongue engages
3. Try to hold that engagement while flattening tongue forward
4. Swallow and try to maintain the position

### Step 4: Maintain 24/7

Mewing only works if you maintain proper posture all day.

**Tips for consistency:**

- Set hourly reminders on phone
- Practice while watching TV, working, walking
- It should become automatic after 2-3 months
- Your tongue may be sore at first (like any new muscle engagement)

## Common Mewing Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake #1: Only Tip Touching

**Fix:** Engage entire tongue. Use swallow technique to find correct position.

### ❌ Mistake #2: Pushing Too Hard

**Fix:** Gentle, constant pressure - not forceful pushing. Think 20-30% effort.

### ❌ Mistake #3: Clenching Teeth

**Fix:** Teeth should barely touch. Jaw should be relaxed.

### ❌ Mistake #4: Mouth Breathing

**Fix:** Keep lips sealed, breathe through nose. See doctor if you can't breathe through nose.

### ❌ Mistake #5: Giving Up Too Soon

**Fix:** Results take 6-24 months. This is a lifestyle change, not a quick fix.

## Hard Mewing vs Soft Mewing

**Soft Mewing (Recommended for Beginners)**

- Gentle, constant pressure
- Sustainable 24/7
- Lower risk of TMJ issues
- Steady, safe progress

**Hard Mewing (Advanced)**

- Forceful tongue pressure
- Done in sessions (10-20 min)
- Faster results but higher risk
- Can cause jaw pain/clicking

**Recommendation:** Start with soft mewing for 6+ months before attempting hard mewing.

## Mewing FAQ

**Q: Is mewing scientifically proven?**
Limited peer-reviewed studies exist, but thousands of anecdotal reports show positive results. It's low-risk, so worth trying.

**Q: Can mewing fix my recessed chin?**
May help improve profile over time, especially if under 25. Significant recession may require orthodontics or surgery.

**Q: How long until I see results?**
Minimum 6 months for subtle changes. 12-24 months for significant transformation.

**Q: Can I mew if I have braces?**
Yes! Actually recommended. Your tongue should be up against palate, not pushing on teeth.

**Q: Will mewing change my face shape?**
May enhance existing features (sharper jaw, better proportions) but won't completely change your base genetics.

**Q: Is mewing dangerous?**
No serious risks if done correctly (gentle pressure). Avoid hard mewing initially.
