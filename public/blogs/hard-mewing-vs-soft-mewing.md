# Hard Mewing vs Soft Mewing: Which Is Better?

Confused about hard mewing vs soft mewing? This guide explains both techniques, shows you which to use, and helps you avoid common mistakes that ruin results.

## What Is Mewing?

**Definition:** Proper tongue posture where the entire tongue rests against the roof of the mouth (palate), supporting forward facial development.

**Origin:** Developed by Dr. Mike Mew, British orthodontist specializing in orthotropics (proper facial growth).

**Goal:** Improve jawline, facial structure, and breathing through consistent tongue posture.

## Soft Mewing Explained

### What It Is

**Soft mewing** is gentle, comfortable tongue posture maintained 24/7 with minimal conscious effort.

**Technique:**

- Tongue rests lightly against palate
- No forceful pressing
- Comfortable, sustainable pressure
- Should feel natural after adaptation period
- Can maintain while talking, eating, sleeping

**Pressure Level:** 2-3/10 intensity

### How to Do Soft Mewing

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Close mouth** (teeth lightly touching or slightly apart)
2. **Swallow** to find natural tongue position
3. **Keep tongue where it lands** after swallow
4. **Entire tongue up** - including back third (most important)
5. **Maintain lightly** - should feel effortless once habitual
6. **Breathe through nose** only

**Key Point:** The back third of tongue against soft palate is critical. Many people only press the front/tip and miss the main benefit.

### Benefits of Soft Mewing

✅ **Sustainable long-term** (can maintain 24/7)
✅ **No TMJ risk** (gentle pressure)
✅ **Proper facial development** over time
✅ **Improved breathing** and nasal airway
✅ **Better sleep quality** (nose breathing)
✅ **No conscious effort** once habitual

**Timeline:** 6-24 months for visible results (younger = faster)

**Best For:**

- Beginners starting mewing
- Long-term facial development
- Anyone under 25 (still growing)
- People prone to TMJ issues

### Soft Mewing Results

**What to Expect:**

- Gradual forward facial growth
- Improved jawline definition (over months)
- Better facial proportions
- Enhanced cheekbone projection
- Straighter teeth (in growing individuals)

**Realistic Impact:** +0.3 to +0.7 points on face rating over 12-24 months

## Hard Mewing Explained

### What It Is

**Hard mewing** is forceful, intentional tongue pressing against the palate with maximum pressure.

**Technique:**

- Tongue pushes hard against roof of mouth
- Active, conscious effort required
- Cannot maintain while talking/eating
- Done in focused sessions (not 24/7)
- Significantly more intense

**Pressure Level:** 8-10/10 intensity

### How to Do Hard Mewing

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Start with soft mewing position**
2. **Press tongue upward forcefully** against palate
3. **Maximum comfortable pressure** (should feel strain)
4. **Hold for 10-30 seconds**
5. **Rest 30-60 seconds**
6. **Repeat** for 10-20 minutes total

**Session Frequency:** 1-2x daily, 10-20 min each

**Warning:** Never do to the point of pain. Discomfort is okay; pain means Stop.

### Benefits of Hard Mewing

✅ **Faster visible results** (weeks to months vs months to years)
✅ **Builds tongue strength** (helps maintain soft mewing)
✅ **More dramatic changes** in shorter time
✅ **Useful for older individuals** (post-growth plate closure)

**Timeline:** 3-9 months for visible results

**Best For:**

- Age 20+ (growth plates closing/closed)
- Impatient individuals wanting faster results
- Those who've mastered soft mewing
- Building tongue muscle strength

### Hard Mewing Risks

⚠️ **TMJ dysfunction** (jaw joint issues)
⚠️ **Teeth shifting** (potentially unwanted movement)
⚠️ **Uneven development** (asymmetry if uneven pressure)
⚠️ **Headaches** (from excessive strain)
⚠️ **Not sustainable 24/7** (can't do constantly)

**Critical:** Hard mewing should NEVER cause pain. If you experience jaw pain, clicking, or headaches, STOP immediately and return to soft mewing only.

## Hard vs Soft Mewing: Direct Comparison

### Pressure & Intensity

**Soft Mewing:**

- Light, gentle pressure
- Feels like tongue"resting" naturally
- 2-3/10 effort
- Sustainable indefinitely

**Hard Mewing:**

- Forceful, active pushing
- Feels like intense exercise
- 8-10/10 effort
- Can only maintain short periods

### Time Commitment

**Soft Mewing:**

- 24/7 (all waking hours + sleep)
- No dedicated "session" needed
- Becomes automatic habit

**Hard Mewing:**

- 10-20 min sessions, 1-2x daily
- Requires focused attention
- Cannot do while talking/eating

### Results Timeline

**Soft Mewing:**

- First changes: 3-6 months
- Visible results: 6-12 months
- Mature results: 12-24 months
- Best results: 2+ years

**Hard Mewing:**

- First changes: 2-weeks to 2 months
- Visible results: 3-6 months
- Mature results: 6-12 months
- Plateau: 12-18 months

### Risk Level

**Soft Mewing:**

- Very low risk
- Virtually no side effects if done correctly
- Safe for all ages
- No TMJ risk

**Hard Mewing:**

- Moderate to high risk
- TMJ dysfunction possible
- Teeth shifting risk
- Asymmetry if uneven pressure
- Headaches common initially

### Sustainability

**Soft Mewing:**

- ✅ Can maintain forever
- ✅ Becomes unconscious habit
- ✅ Do while sleeping
- ✅ Compatible with daily life

**Hard Mewing:**

- ❌ Cannot do 24/7
- ❌ Requires conscious effort
- ❌ Interferes with talking/eating
- ❌ Fatiguing over time

## Which Should You Choose?

### Start with Soft Mewing If

- You're under 25 (still experiencing facial growth)
- You're a complete beginner
- You have TMJ issues or jaw pain
- You want safe, sustainable results
- You're patient and thinking long-term

### Add Hard Mewing If

- You're over 20 and growth plates are closed
- You've mastered soft mewing (6+ months experience)
- You want faster results
- You have no TMJ issues
- You're willing to accept moderate risk

### Never Do Hard Mewing If

- You have existing TMJ dysfunction
- You experience jaw clicking or pain
- You're prone to teeth grinding (bruxism)
- You haven't mastered soft mewing first
- You're under 18 (unnecessary - soft mewing is enough)

## The Optimal Mewing Strategy

### Recommended Approach

**Phase 1: Master Soft Mewing (Months 1-6)**

- Focus 100% on soft mewing
- Build tongue strength naturally
- Develop proper habit (24/7 tongue up)
- Allow natural facial changes
- Monitor for any issues

**Phase 2: Evaluate (Month 6)**

- Take progress photos
- Compare to baseline
- Assess if soft mewing alone is sufficient
- Decide if hard mewing worth the risk

**Phase 3: Add Hard Mewing (Month 7+ - Optional)**

- Continue soft mewing 24/7 (foundation)
- Add 10-min hard mewing session daily
- Start conservatively (5 min, 3x/week)
- Gradually increase if no issues
- Stop immediately if pain develops

**Phase 4: Maintenance (Year 2+)**

- Soft mewing remains forever
- Hard mewing as needed for boosts
- Monitor and adjust based on results

### The 80/20 Rule

**80% of results come from soft mewing.**
Hard mewing may add the final 20% but comes with risk.

**Priority:** Master soft mewing first. It's the foundation.

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake #1: Skipping Soft Mewing, Going Straight to Hard

**Why it's bad:** No foundation, high injury risk, can't maintain proper form

**Fix:** Spend minimum 3-6 months mastering soft mewing before attempting hard mewing

### ❌ Mistake #2: Only Pressing Front of Tongue

**Why it's bad:** The back third against soft palate is most important for facial development

**Fix:** Focus on getting the ENTIRE tongue up, especially the posterior third

### ❌ Mistake #3: Doing Hard Mewing 24/7

**Why it's bad:** Unsustainable, causes TMJ, leads to injury

**Fix:** Hard mewing is for SHORT sessions (10-20 min max). Soft mewing is for 24/7.

### ❌ Mistake #4: Inconsistent Soft Mewing

**Why it's bad:** Results require constant pressure over months/years. Doing 2 hours/day won't work.

**Fix:** Soft mewing must become an unconscious 24/7 habit including sleep.

### ❌ Mistake #5: Continuing Through Pain

**Why it's bad:** Pain indicates injury developing (TMJ, muscle strain)

**Fix:** Any pain = stop hard mewing immediately. Soft mewing should never hurt.

### ❌ Mistake #6: Expecting Overnight Results

**Why it's bad:** Facial remodeling takes months minimum. Quitting after 2 weeks means zero results.

**Fix:** Commit to 12 months minimum. Track with monthly photos, not daily mirror staring.

## Age Considerations

### Under 18

**Recommendation:** Soft mewing ONLY

- Still growing, bones are malleable
- Soft mewing is sufficient
- Hard mewing unnecessary and risky
- Results come faster naturally

### Ages 18-25

**Recommendation:** Soft mewing + light hard mewing (optional)

- Growth plates closing but still some growth
- Soft mewing should be primary
- Light hard mewing can accelerate (3-5/10 pressure)
- Monitor closely for issues

### Ages 25-30

**Recommendation:** Soft mewing + hard mewing (if experienced)

- Growth plates closed
- Harder to see changes
- Hard mewing may be necessary for visible results
- Higher risk - be cautious

### Over 30

**Recommendation:** Soft mewing + hard mewing + realistic expectations

- Minimal bone remodeling possible
- Benefits mostly breathing/sleep
- Hard mewing for marginal jawline enhancement
- Consider surgical options if major changes desired

## Tracking Your Progress

### How to Monitor Results

**Before Photos (Baseline):**

- Front facing (neutral expression, eye level)
- Side profile (ear to chin visible)
- 45-degree angle
- Same lighting, same time of day

**Monthly Progress Photos:**

- Exact same position/lighting as baseline
- Compare side-by-side
- Look for subtle changes (jawline, midface, cheekbones)

**Measurements:**

- Gonial angle (jaw angle with protractor on photo)
- Forward facial growth (profile comparison)
- Jawline definition (visual assessment)

**Subjective Markers:**

- Breathing quality (easier nasal breathing?)
- Sleep quality (less snoring/apnea?)
- Facial tension (less jaw clenching?)
- Teeth alignment (straighter over time?)

### Realistic Expectations

**Soft Mewing (24 months):**

- Jawline: Slight to moderate improvement
- Midface: Minor forward growth
- Cheekbones: Subtle enhancement
- Overall: +0.3 to +0.7 points

**Soft + Hard Mewing (12-18 months):**

- Jawline: Moderate improvement
- Midface: Noticeable forward growth
- Cheekbones: Visible enhancement
- Overall: +0.5 to +1.0 points

**Not Realistic:**

- Transforming 3/10 face to 8/10 (mewing can't change bone structure that drastically)
- Seeing major changes in weeks (takes months minimum)
- Fixing severe recession without surgery (mewing has limits)

## Bottom Line: Which Is Better?

**Short Answer:** Soft mewing is better for 95% of people.

**Why:**

- Sustainable lifelong
- Low risk
- Proper facial development
- Can do 24/7
- Becomes automatic

**Hard mewing is supplementary, not primary.**

**The Ideal Combination:**

- Soft mewing 24/7 (foundation) = 80% of results
- Hard mewing 10 min/day (optional boost) = 20% of results

Start with soft mewing. Master it for 6+ months. Then decide if hard mewing is worth the added risk for potentially faster (but not necessarily better) results.
