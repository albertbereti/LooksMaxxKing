# Canthal Tilt Guide: Hunter Eyes vs Prey Eyes (2026)

Canthal tilt is one of the most important markers of eye attractiveness. This guide explains positive vs negative tilt, how to measure yours, and what you can actually change.

## What Is Canthal Tilt?

**Definition:** The angle formed by the outer corner of your eye relative to the inner corner

**Three Types:**

1. **Positive Canthal Tilt:** Outer corner higher than inner (attractive)
2. **Neutral Canthal Tilt:** Outer and inner at same level (average)
3. **Negative Canthal Tilt:** Outer corner lower than inner (unattractive)

**Impact on Attractiveness:**

- Positive tilt: +0.5 to +1.0 points
- Neutral tilt: Baseline (0 points)
- Negative tilt: -0.4 to -0.8 points

**Why It Matters:**

- Signals youth (eyes droop with age)
- Perceived as more aggressive/dominant (masculine)
- Creates "hunter eyes" look
- Associated with facial attractiveness in studies

## Hunter Eyes vs Prey Eyes

### Hunter Eyes (Ideal)

**Characteristics:**

- **Positive canthal tilt** (outer corner 5-10° higher)
- Deep-set eyes (recessed into skull)
- Hooded upper eyelid (brow bone coverage)
- Almond shape
- Minimal scleral show (white of eye)
- Strong brow ridge

**Examples:**

- Henry Cavill
- Sean O'Pry
- Brad Pitt
- Adriana Lima (women can have hunter eyes too)

**Effect:** Masculine, attractive, predatory gaze

### Prey Eyes (Unattractive)

**Characteristics:**

- **Negative canthal tilt** (outer corner lower)
- Round, wide eyes (bug-eyed)
- Lots of visible sclera (sanpaku eyes)
- Shallow-set eyes (bulging out)
- Weak brow ridge

**Effect:** Feminine (on men), fearful appearance, less attractive

**Impact:** -0.6 to -1.2 points on attractiveness

## How to Measure Your Canthal Tilt

### Method 1: Photo Analysis

1. Take straight-on photo (eye level)
2. Draw line from inner corner to outer corner of each eye
3. Measure angle relative to horizontal

**Positive:** Line slopes upward (outer > inner)
**Neutral:** Line is horizontal
**Negative:** Line slopes downward (outer < inner)

### Method 2: Mirror + Straight Edge

1. Face mirror straight-on

## What Is Canthal Tilt Determined By?

### Bone Structure (90% Genetic)

**Orbital Bone Shape:**

- The bones surrounding your eye socket determine tilt
- Lateral orbital rim position = outer eye corner height
- This is 100% genetic, set by age 5-6

**Zygomatic Bones (Cheekbones):**

- High, forward cheekbones support outer eye corner
- Lifts canthal tilt naturally
- Flat cheekbones = less support = neutral/negative tilt

**Maxilla Position:**

- Forward maxilla (undereye support) = positive tilt
- Recessed maxilla = negative tilt tendency

**Bottom Line:** Born with it. Can't change without surgery.

### Soft Tissue (10%)

**Skin Laxity:**

- Aging = skin sags = tilt becomes less positive
- Sun damage accelerates
- Collagen loss

**Muscle Tone:**

- Weak orbicularis oculi = drooping
- Strength doesn't change bones but affects appearance

**Temporary Factors:**

- Swelling, water retention, allergies
- Can make tilt appear worse temporarily

## Can You Change Your Canthal Tilt?

### Non-Surgical: VERY LIMITED

**What Might Help (Marginally):**

1. **Mewing** (if under 25)
   - Forward maxilla growth MIGHT support orbital region better
   - 0-2° improvement max (years of mewing)
   - Don't expect major change

2. **Cheekbone Development**
   - Bone smashing (controversial, not proven)
   - No scientific evidence
   - Risk of damage

3. **Facial Muscle Training**
   - Squinting exercises (strengthens muscles)
   - No bone changes
   - +0-1° improvement max

4. **Body Fat Reduction**
   - Reveals bone structure
   - Doesn't change tilt but enhances visibility
   - Especially if high cheekbones masked by fat

**REALITY:** Non-surgical changes to canthal tilt are minimal to non-existent.

### Surgical: EFFECTIVE (But Risky)

**Canthoplasty (Lateral Canthoplasty)**

- Surgically repositions outer eye corner
- Can increase tilt by 5-10°
- Transforms negative → positive

**Cost:** $3,000-8,0 (per eye or both)

**Recovery:** 2-4 weeks visible swelling

**Risks:**

- Scarring
- Asymmetry
- Ectropion (eyelid doesn't close properly)
- Infection
- Unnatural appearance if overdone

**Effectiveness:** 80-90% satisfaction in studies

**Who It's For:**

- Severe negative canthal tilt (-5° or worse)
- Major impact on appearance
- Realistic expectations
- Good surgeon critical

### Makeup/Illusion (Temporary)

**For Photos/Events:**

- Winged eyeliner (extends upward) = fakes positive tilt
- Highlighter on cheekbones = lifts appearance
- Not a real solution but works for pictures

## Other Eye Features That Matter

### Pupillary Distance (IPD)

**What It Is:** Space between pupils

**Ideal:**

- Men: 64-68mm
- Women: 62-66mm
- Too wide OR too narrow = less attractive

**Can't Change:** 100% genetic

### Eyebrow-Eye Distance

**What It Is:** Space between eyebrow and eye

**Ideal:**

- Men: Closer (low-set brows, more hooded)
- Women: Slightly more space

**How to Improve:**

- Eyebrow grooming (tweeze below brow only)
- Lose face fat (reduces upper eyelid fat)

### Upper Eyelid Exposure

**What It Is:** How much upper eyelid is visible

**Ideal:**

- Minimal (hooded eyes = attractive)
- Excessive = bug-eyed

**How to Improve:**

- Lose face fat
- Upper blepharoplasty (removes excess skin - $3K-6K)

## Canthal Tilt Surgery (Canthoplasty) Deep Dive

### Types of Canthoplasty

**1. Lateral Canthoplasty**

- Tightens/repositions outer corner
- +3° to +8° improvement
- Most common

**2. Canthopexy**

- Less aggressive, tightens without cutting tendon
- +2° to +5° improvement
- Lower risk but less dramatic

**3. Medial Canthoplasty**

- Adjusts inner corner (reduces IPD)
- Rarely done for aesthetics

### Best Surgeons (Specialization Matters)

**Top Locations:**

- South Korea (world leaders in eye surgery)
- USA (board-certified oculoplastic surgeons)
- Turkey (cheaper, variable quality)

**What to Look For:**

- Oculoplastic surgeon (NOT general plastic surgeon)
- 100+ canthoplasty procedures
- Before/after photos of men (not just women)
- Realistic expectations discussion

### Cost Breakdown

**South Korea:** $2,000-4,000 (highest skill)
**USA:** $4,000-8,000 (convenient, safe)
**Turkey:** $1,500-3,000 (cheaper, research surgeon well)

**Additional Costs:**

- Travel (if abroad): $1,000-2,000
- Recovery time off: 1-2 weeks

**Total Investment:** $3,000-10,000

### Recovery Timeline

**Week 1:** Severe swelling, bruising (stay home)
**Week 2:** Visible swelling decreasing (can go out with sunglasses)
**Week 3-4:** Most swelling gone, results emerging
**Month 2-3:** Final results visible
**Month 6:** Complete healing

### Risks to Consider

- **Ectropion:** Eyelid pulls away from eye (1-2% risk)
- **Asymmetry:** One eye higher than other (revision needed)
- **Scarring:** Usually hidden but possible
- **Overcorrection:** Too much tilt (unnatural)

**How to Minimize:**

- Choose experienced surgeon
- Don't go for maximum tilt (conservative is better)
- Follow post-op care religiously
