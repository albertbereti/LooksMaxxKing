# Bone Structure and Attractiveness: What You Can (and Can't) Change

Your bone structure is the foundation of facial attractiveness. This guide explains which bones matter most, what's genetic, and what's actually improvable.

## Why Bone Structure Is King

**The Hierarchy of Attractiveness:**

1. **Bone structure** (40% of rating) - Foundation
2. Facial proportions (25%) - Built on bones
3. Skin quality (15%) - Surface layer
4. Symmetry (10%) - Bone-dependent
5. Grooming/style (10%) - Enhancement

**Truth:** You can have perfect skin and grooming but weak bones = average at best.

**Good News:** Some bones CAN be improved non-surgically (mewing, body fat). Most requires surgery but understanding what you're working with is critical.

## The Bones That Matter Most

### 1. Maxilla (Upper Jaw) - MOST IMPORTANT

**What It Is:** Upper jaw bone that holds your teeth

**Why It Matters:**

- Determines midface projection (forward vs flat face)
- Affects cheekbone prominence
- Controls under-eye support (prevents hollow look)
- Impacts nasal aesthetics
- **Accounts for 30% of facial attractiveness alone**

**Ideal Maxilla:**

- Projects forward (not recessed)
- Wide enough for all teeth
- High cheekbones as result
- Supports eyes well

**Poor Maxilla (Recessed):**

- Flat midface (no forward projection)
- Sunken appearance
- Long face
- Mouth breathing often the cause

**Can You Change It?**

- **Mewing (Under 25):** Yes, 3-15mm forward growth possible over 2-5 years
- **Surgery:** Yes, LeFort I, BIMAX, SARPE
- **Body fat reduction:** Reveals existing structure but doesn't change bones

### 2. Mandible (Lower Jaw/Jawline)

**What It Is:** Lower jaw bone, creates your jawline

**Why It Matters:**

- Defines jawline sharpness
- Creates masculine appearance
- Affects profile aesthetics
- **Accounts for 25% of facial attractiveness**

**Ideal Mandible:**

- Wide jaw angles (gonial angle ~120-125°)
- Forward chin projection
- Defined jawline (low gonial angle visible)
- Proportional to upper face

**Poor Mandible:**

- Recessed chin (weak profile)
- Narrow jaw (no width)
- High gonial angle (long lower face)
- "No jawline"

**Can You Change It?**

- **Mewing:** Slight improvements (jaw width, forward growth)
- **Body Fat:** Massive impact (reveals jawline if it exists)
- **Chewing:** Minimal (strengthens masseters, not bones)
- **Surgery:** Genioplasty, jaw implants, BSSO

### 3. Zygomatic Bones (Cheekbones)

**What They Are:** Bones creating cheek prominence

**Why They Matter:**

- High cheekbones = attractive (both sexes)
- Create facial angularity
- Affect midface width
- **Accounts for 15% of facial attractiveness**

**Ideal Zygomatic Bones:**

- High placement (closer to eyes than mouth)
- Forward projection (not flat)
- Wide (adds face width)
- Visible angularity

**Poor Zygomatic Bones:**

- Low, flat cheekbones
- No projection
- Round, soft face

**Can You Change Them?**

- **Mewing:** Minimal (maxilla changes affect cheekbone appearance)
- **Body Fat:** Significant reveal (hidden by face fat)
- **Surgery:** Cheek implants, zygomatic osteotomy

### 4. Orbital Bones (Eye Sockets)

**What They Are:** Bones surrounding eyes

**Why They Matter:**

- Determine eye depth (hunter vs prey eyes)
- Affect brow ridge prominence
- Control under-eye support
- **Accounts for 10% of facial attractiveness**

**Ideal Orbital Bones:**

- Deep-set (eyes recessed into skull)
- Strong brow ridge (hooding effect)
- Minimal scleral show
- Positive canthal tilt support

**Poor Orbital Bones:**

- Shallow (eyes bulge out)
- Weak brow ridge
- Under-eye hollowness

**Can You Change Them?**

- **Mewing/Maxilla:** Indirect improvement (maxilla supports orbitals)
- **Body Fat:** Minor impact
- **Surgery:** Orbital rim implants, orbital decompression (extreme)

### 5. Nasal Bones

**What They Are:** Bones forming nose structure

**Why They Matter:**

- Central facial feature
- Affects profile
- Proportions critical
- **Accounts for 10% of facial attractiveness**

**Ideal Nasal Bones:**

- Straight bridge
- Proportional to face size
- Upturned tip (slight)
- Narrow from front

**Poor Nasal Bones:**

- Crooked, deviated
- Too wide or bulbous
- Hook nose
- Flat bridge

**Can You Change Them?**

- **Mewing:** No direct effect on nose bones
- **Surgery:** Rhinoplasty

## Genetic vs Improvable Bone Structure

### 100% Genetic (Can't Change Without Surgery)

**Skull Shape:**

- Head width, length, shape
- Facial thirds ratios
- Bone density/thickness

**Eye Spacing:**

- Interpupillary distance
- Canthal tilt (bone-determined)
- Eye socket size

**Maxilla Width:**

- Dental arch width (past puberty)
- Palate width (without SARPE surgery)

### Partially Improvable (Mewing, Lifestyle)

**Maxilla Forward Growth:**

- Mewing can move it forward 3-15mm (if under 25)
- Older: 0-5mm possible with years of mewing

**Mandible Position:**

- Mewing improves forward position
- Chewing strengthens muscles (not bones much)

**Cheekbone Prominence:**

- Revealed by low body fat
- Indirectly improved by maxilla forward growth

### Highly Improvable (Non-Surgical)

**Jawline Visibility:**

- Body fat 10-12% reveals jawline
- Can go from "no jawline" to defined (+1.0 rating points)

**Facial Angularity:**

- Body fat reduction creates angles
- Masculine appearance increases dramatically

**Under-Eye Area:**

- Maxilla support improves dark circles
- Sleep, hydration, body fat all factor

## How to Assess Your Bone Structure

### The Mirror Test

**Front View:**

1. **Face Width:** Measure across cheekbones - wide is better
2. **Jaw Width:** Measure at jaw angles - should match cheekbones
3. **Cheekbone Height:** High (close to eyes) vs low?
4. **Jawline Definition:** Can you see it or hidden by fat?

**Side Profile:**

1. **Maxilla Projection:** Face flat or forward?
2. **Chin Projection:** Aligns with forehead or recessed?
3. **Nose-to-Lips:** Proportional?
4. **Brow Ridge:** Prominent or flat?

**45° Angle:**

1. **Cheekbone Projection:** Do they cast shadow?
2. **Jaw Angle:** Visible and sharp or rounded?

### Professional Assessment

**Best Method:**

- [AI Face Rating Analysis](https://looksmaxxking.com) - instant bone structure breakdown
- **Orthodontist/Oral Surgeon:** Cephalometric X-ray (shows bone positions)
- **Facial Plastic Surgeon:** 3D CT scan consultation

### Key Measurements

**Golden Ratio Checks:**

- Face length / face width ≈ 1.618
- Midface length / lower face ≈ 1:1
- Jaw width / cheekbone width ≈ 0.9-1.0

## Improving Bone Structure (Non-Surgical)

### 1. Mewing (Ages 13-25 Most Effective)

**What It Does:**

- Moves maxilla forward 3-15mm over years
- Widens palate (if under 18)
- Improves facial proportions
- Enhances cheekbone appearance

**How Long:**

- 12-24 months: First changes
- 2-5 years: Significant transformation
- Lifelong: Maintains results

**Realistic Expectations:**

- Under 18: Dramatic changes possible
- 18-25: Moderate improvements
- Over 25: Minimal bone changes (mostly posture)

**[Full Mewing Tutorial →](https://looksmaxxking.com/blogs/mewing-tutorial-beginners)**

### 2. Body Fat Reduction (BIGGEST IMPACT)

**What It Does:**

- Reveals hidden bone structure
- Creates jawline definition
- Enhances cheekbones
- Sharpens facial angles

**Target:**

- Men: 10-12% body fat
- Women: 18-20% body fat

**Impact:**

- Can improve face rating by +0.8 to +1.5 points
- Often reveals good bones you didn't know you had

**Timeline:** 3-6 months from average starting point

**[Body Fat & Facial Aesthetics Guide →](https://looksmaxxking.com/blogs/body-fat-facial-aesthetics)**

### 3. Chewing Hard Foods (Minimal Bone Impact)

**What It Does:**

- Builds masseter muscles (jawline muscles)
- Adds width to lower face
- **Does NOT change bones significantly**

**Methods:**

- Chew gum (sugar-free, hard)
- Chew mastic gum (very hard)
- Eat tough foods (steak, nuts)

**Realistic Expectations:**

- +5-10mm jaw width from muscle
- Minimal bone changes
- Can make square faces too wide

### 4. Posture Correction

**What It Does:**

- Proper head/neck alignment
- Makes existing bone structure more visible
- Prevents bone recession over time

**Key Points:**

- Head directly above shoulders
- Chin slightly tucked
- Tongue on palate
- Shoulders back

**Impact:** Immediate visual improvement, prevents worsening

## Surgical Options (Permanent Changes)

### Maxillofacial Surgeries

**1. LeFort I Osteotomy**

- **What:** Moves entire maxilla (upper jaw) forward/up/down
- **Best For:** Recessed midface, flat profile
- **Cost:** $20,000-40,000
- **Recovery:** 6-12 weeks
- **Impact:** +1.5 to +2.5 rating points (most dramatic)

**2. BIMAX (Bimaxillary Surgery)**

- **What:** Moves both upper and lower jaws
- **Best For:** Severe recession, bite issues
- **Cost:** $30,000-60,000
- **Recovery:** 3-6 months
- **Impact:** Complete facial transformation

**3. SARPE (Palate Expansion)**

- **What:** Widens maxilla/palate
- **Best For:** Narrow face, crowded teeth
- **Cost:** $5,000-15,000
- **Recovery:** 3-6 months
- **Impact:** +0.5 to +1.0 points (face width)

### Implants & Augmentation

**1. Cheek Implants**

- **Cost:** $3,000-6,000 per side
- **Recovery:** 2-4 weeks
- **Impact:** +0.5 to +0.8 points

**2. Jaw Implants**

- **Cost:** $5,000-10,000
- **Recovery:** 2-4 weeks
- **Impact:** +0.6 to +1.0 points

**3. Chin Implant (Genioplasty)**

- **Cost:** $3,000-7,000
- **Recovery:** 2-3 weeks
- **Impact:** +0.4 to +0.8 points

**4. Brow Bone/Orbital Rim Implants**

- **Cost:** $5,000-10,000
- **Recovery:** 2-4 weeks
- **Impact:** +0.3 to +0.6 points

## Bone Structure Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake #1: Ignoring Body Fat

**Why Bad:** Assuming poor bones when it's just high body fat hiding them.

**Fix:** Get to 10-12% body fat first, THEN assess bones.

### ❌ Mistake #2: Mewing with Terrible Posture

**Why Bad:** Mewing requires proper head/neck posture. Forward head negates benefits.

**Fix:** Fix posture first, then mew.

### ❌ Mistake #3: Expecting Fast Results

**Why Bad:** Bone remodeling takes 1-5 years. Quitting at month 6 wastes effort.

**Fix:** Commit to long-term mewing, track yearly progress.

### ❌ Mistake #4: Chewing Instead of Mewing

**Why Bad:** Chewing builds muscle, not bone. Mewing changes bone structure.

**Fix:** Mewing is the foundation. Chewing is optional supplementation.

### ❌ Mistake #5: Surgery Too Early

**Why Bad:** If under 25, bones still developing. Surgery locks you in.

**Fix:** Exhaust non-surgical methods first. Surgery is last resort (or fast track if wealthy).
